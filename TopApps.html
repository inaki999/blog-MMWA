🛍️ Selling Your Extra Stuff

Turning clutter into cash is still the quickest win this year. Use eBay for things you can ship anywhere, Wallapop for getting rid of bulky items locally without shipping costs, and Vinted if you have a closet full of clothes you don't wear. Just snap a clear photo and you're in business.

💳 How you get paid: Direct transfers to your bank, PayPal balances, or cold hard cash when meeting in person.

🔥 What's selling fastest right now:

  • Modern Phones & Tablets
  • Old Nintendo & PlayStation gear
  • Limited Edition Trainers
  • 90s and Y2K Style Clothes
  • Laptops & MacBooks
  • TCG (Pokémon/Magic)
  • Kitchen Tech (Air Fryers)
  • LEGO Sets
  • Minimalist Furniture
  • Dumbbells & Home Gym Gear
🗣️
Tutoring

italki

If you're fluent in any language, you can get paid to talk. italki lets you sign up as a professional teacher or just a community tutor. You don't need a degree to help people practice conversation, and you can work from your couch.

Pros: You decide your own rates and you're not forced into a set schedule.
Cons: It takes a while to stand out if you teach a very common language.
Payout: Payoneer, PayPal, or Bank. Cash out at $30.
✍️
Translation

Gengo

Gengo is great for people who are bilingual and want to do quick translation jobs. Once you pass their test, you can pick up tasks from their dashboard whenever you have free time.

Pros: Plenty of work available if you speak major languages. The interface is very clean.
Cons: The pay per word is fixed, so it's lower than what a private client might pay.
Payout: PayPal or Payoneer. Paid twice a month.
🎓
Academic Research

Prolific

Unlike regular survey sites that screen you out mid-way, Prolific only shows you studies you actually qualify for. It's run by researchers, so the questions are actually interesting and the pay is fair.

Pros: No disqualifications once you start. Reliable, honest pay.
Cons: They are very strict about quality; one wrong move and you're out.
Payout: PayPal. Cash out starts at £6.
💻
Website Testing

UserTesting

Companies want to know why people struggle to use their websites. You get paid to record your screen and speak your thoughts aloud while navigating an app or a site. It's actually quite fun.

Pros: High pay—$10 for a 20-minute test is standard.
Cons: You have to be fast to grab tests before they disappear.
Payout: PayPal. Paid 7 days after the test.
🤖
AI Training

Remotasks

Help teach AI how to see the world. You’ll be labeling images or identifying objects in 3D videos. It’s simple work but requires a lot of focus to get the labels right.

Pros: Weekly payments and you can start with zero experience.
Cons: Some of the training modules can be a bit tedious.
Payout: PayPal or Airtm. Paid every Tuesday.
💼
Freelancing

Upwork

Upwork is where you go if you have a real skill like coding, writing, or design. It’s a massive marketplace where you can find everything from one-off tasks to full-time remote roles.

Pros: You can build a real career here with high earnings.
Cons: You have to pay for "connects" to bid on the best jobs.
Payout: Bank Transfer, PayPal, or Payoneer.
👟
Walking App

Sweatcoin

Sweatcoin pays you for your daily steps. In 2026, it's a solid way to earn their crypto token (SWEAT) which you can trade or use to buy products in their shop.

Pros: Set it and forget it. It runs in the background.
Cons: You won't get rich walking; it's a slow earner.
Payout: SWEAT Tokens or Store Rewards.
🎙️
Audio Typing

Rev

If you type fast, Rev is a solid choice. You listen to interviews or videos and type out what’s being said. It's flexible—just log in whenever you want to work.

Pros: There is almost always work available to claim.
Cons: The pay is per minute of audio, so you need to be efficient.
Payout: PayPal. Paid every single Monday.
🛍️
Cashback

Rakuten

This is basically a rebate program for things you were going to buy anyway. Use their link when you shop at places like Amazon or Walmart, and they give you a cut of the commission.

Pros: No effort required other than clicking a link before you buy.
Cons: They only send out payments every quarter.
Payout: PayPal or Check.
📸
Photography

Shutterstock

Instead of letting your photos sit in your gallery, upload them here. If a designer or a blog uses your photo, you get a royalty. It’s passive income that grows as you add more content.

Pros: A single photo can keep making you money for years.
Cons: They have high standards for technical quality.
Payout: PayPal, Skrill, or Payoneer.
🔨
Local Labor

TaskRabbit

If you're handy with a drill or good at moving boxes, TaskRabbit is the place to be. You pick up local jobs like fixing shelves or helping people move house.

Pros: You can charge $30-$50 per hour depending on the task.
Cons: Only available in major cities and it's physical work.
Payout: Bank Transfer.
📊
Microtasks

Swagbucks

The "everything" app. You can earn points for searching the web, watching ads, or playing games. It’s been around forever and it’s very reliable for small amounts of extra cash.

Pros: Dozens of ways to earn; very low payout threshold.
Cons: The pay per task is quite low.
Payout: PayPal or massive selection of Gift Cards.
🌐
Passive Sharing

Honeygain

Honeygain lets companies use your unused internet connection for market research. You just install the app and it runs quietly. It won't slow you down, and you earn for doing nothing.

Pros: Truly passive. No interaction needed after setup.
Cons: It takes a few months to reach the $20 payout.
Payout: PayPal or JMPT (Crypto).
🎨
Design

99designs

Enter design contests for logos, websites, and more. If the client picks your design, you win the prize money. It's a great way to build a portfolio and land big clients.

Pros: High payouts for contest winners.
Cons: You might work on a design and not get paid if you don't win.
Payout: PayPal or Payoneer.
📍
Local Missions

Premise

Get paid for being a local contributor. You might be asked to check if a local store has a certain product or take a photo of a landmark. It's simple and gets you out of the house.

Pros: Quick tasks that you can do while running errands.
Cons: GPS usage can be a bit heavy on your battery.
Payout: PayPal, Bitcoin, or Mobile Credit.
🚀
Gig Market

Fiverr

Create a "gig" for anything you can do—from voiceovers to SEO advice. Fiverr is great because clients come to you, rather than you having to hunt them down.

Pros: You set your own prices and packages.
Cons: Fiverr takes a 20% cut of what you earn.
Payout: PayPal or Bank Transfer.
🐶
Pet Services

Rover

If you like dogs, this is the best app out there. You can offer dog walking, pet sitting, or even boarding in your own home. You set your own rules and fees.

Pros: High earning potential if you build a good reputation.
Cons: You have to pay a fee to Rover for every booking.
Payout: Direct Bank Deposit.
🏢
Expert Interviews

Respondent

This is for professionals. Research companies pay well to talk to people with specific industry experience. We're talking $100+ for an hour-long Zoom call.

Pros: Extremely high pay compared to any other survey site.
Cons: It's hard to find studies that fit your exact job title.
Payout: PayPal.
📱
Selling Tech

Decluttr

Don't want to deal with eBay buyers? Decluttr gives you an instant price for your old tech. If you agree, you ship it to them for free and they pay you as soon as they check it.

Pros: Zero hassle. No waiting for someone to buy your item.
Cons: You'll get slightly less than you would selling it privately.
Payout: PayPal or Bank Transfer.
🎮
Games

Mistplay

Only for Android users—this app pays you to try out new mobile games. The longer you play, the more "units" you earn. It’s perfect if you spend time gaming anyway.

Pros: Genuinely fun way to earn gift cards while gaming.
Cons: Earning speed drops after you play the same game for a while.
Payout: Amazon, Google Play, or Visa Cards.
🧩
Small Tasks

JumpTask

JumpTask is a micro-job platform where you get paid in crypto. Tasks are tiny—like checking a link or a short test—and the pay is instant.

Pros: No minimum amount to cash out. You get paid right away.
Cons: You need a basic understanding of crypto wallets.
Payout: JMPT (Crypto).
✉️
Rewards

InboxDollars

Similar to Swagbucks, but they pay in actual dollars instead of points. You can earn by reading emails, taking surveys, or scanning receipts.

Pros: Very clear earnings and a nice sign-up bonus.
Cons: You need to reach $15 before you can withdraw.
Payout: PayPal or Gift Cards.
📈
Google Surveys

Google Opinion Rewards

These are the shortest surveys on the internet. Usually 2 or 3 questions about a shop you visited. It only takes a few seconds and the money adds up over time.

Pros: Zero effort. Surveys are extremely quick.
Cons: You might only get a couple of surveys a week.
Payout: PayPal (iOS) or Play Store Credit (Android).
💎
Trying Apps

FeaturePoints

Download free apps, try them for a minute, and earn points. It’s a good way to find new apps and get a little something back for your time.

Pros: Their referral program is excellent if you have friends.
Cons: Some of the best offers require you to reach a certain level in a game.
Payout: PayPal, Bitcoin, or Gift Cards.
📶
Data Sharing

Pawns.app

Like Honeygain, but with a lower payout limit. You can share your internet or do surveys to boost your earnings. It’s a global platform that’s easy to use.

Pros: You only need $5 to cash out. Works worldwide.
Cons: Passive income isn't high, but it's consistent.
Payout: PayPal, Bitcoin, or Visa.
🌍
Surveys

ySense

ySense is a huge platform with multiple survey walls and tasks. It’s been around for over a decade, so you know it’s a site you can actually trust.

Pros: Very reliable with lots of ways to earn.
Cons: Some surveys are long and can be a bit boring.
Payout: PayPal, Amazon, or Payoneer.
🤖
Data Tasks

Toloka

Quick micro-tasks to help train AI models. You might be checking if two images are the same or if a search result makes sense. The tasks are fast and varied.

Pros: You can cash out almost instantly. Work is always available.
Cons: You have to be careful not to make mistakes or you'll lose access to tasks.
Payout: PayPal or Payoneer.
🎮
Gaming Rewards

Freecash

The top-rated site for gamers right now. You get paid to reach certain levels in mobile games. The payouts can be surprisingly high for some of the tougher challenges.

Pros: Daily bonuses and very fast payouts. Massive variety.
Cons: Some games take a lot of time to reach the required level.
Payout: PayPal, Crypto, or Amazon.
👷
CrowdWork

Clickworker

Access the UHRS system through Clickworker. It’s professional micro-work—helping search engines understand the web better. It’s more serious than a regular survey site.

Pros: Stable work and a professional company.
Cons: You have to pass an English assessment to get the best work.
Payout: PayPal or Bank Transfer.
👁️
Honest Surveys

PaidViewpoint

The most honest survey site. They don't have "screeners"—if they invite you to a survey, you get to finish it and get paid. No time wasting here.

Pros: 100% completion rate. No disqualifications.
Cons: You won't get many surveys until you build up your "TraitScore".
Payout: PayPal.
⚙️
Microjobs

Microworkers

Small, quick jobs like testing a sign-up form or checking a link. It’s a bit of an older site, but there are always hundreds of tasks ready to do.

Pros: High volume of work available 24/7.
Cons: The site design is very dated.
Payout: PayPal or Skrill.
📊
Pure Surveys

Survey Junkie

If you just want surveys and nothing else, this is it. It’s a simple, no-frills platform that does exactly what it says on the tin.

Pros: Clean interface and very easy to use.
Cons: You will still get screened out of some surveys occasionally.
Payout: PayPal or Gift Cards.
